Land, where nature reigns supreme and every landscape tells a story of rugged beauty and ancient wonder. From the towering glaciers and thundering waterfalls to the fiery volcanoes and mystical Northern Lights, Iceland offers a captivating blend of adventure and tranquility for travelers seeking the extraordinary

Embark on epic hikes through pristine national parks, dive into crystal-clear glacial waters, or set sail on the North Atlantic in search of majestic whales. Whether you're exploring volcanic landscapes, relaxing in geothermal hot springs, or immersing yourself in Viking history and culture, Iceland promises a journey of discovery unlike any other.

Bjarnarhöfn Shark Museum

The Bjarnahöfn Shark Museum is a fascinating exhibition on the history and processing of hakarl, the traditional Icelandic delicacy of fermented shark.

Adress

Bjarnarhöfn, 341 Stykkishólmi

Blue Lagoon

The Blue Lagoon is a geothermal spa located in southwestern Iceland, near the town of Grindavik. A popular tourist attraction, known for its milky-blue waters, which are rich in minerals and said to have healing properties.

Adress

Norðurljósavegur 9, 240 Grindavík

LEARN MORE

Whale Watching Hauganes

Whale Watching Hauganes is the oldest whale watching tour operator in Iceland and specialising in whale watching and sea angling. Enjoy whale watching in Eyjafjörður fjord and have your catch filleted to your heart's desire.

Adress

Hafnargata 2, 621 Hauganes

LEARN MORE

Glaumbær Farm & Museum

Glaumbær Museum is Iceland’s best museum housed inside turf-roofed farm buildings. Discover traditional Icelandic homes dating back to the 18th and 19th centuries. Take a walk through hobbit-like rooms connected by a central passageway.

Adress

Glaumbær, 561 Varmahlíð
